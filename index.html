📊 Extraction and Analysis of Multi-modal Dataset for Mental Disorder Detection

This study integrated multiple modalities to investigate neurophysiological and cognitive markers of depression.

Combined EEG recordings (resting state and an emotional dot-probe task) with behavioral performance measures and clinical questionnaires.

EEG data were processed to extract features such as power spectral density, event-related potentials, and time–frequency representations.

Reaction times and accuracy from cognitive tasks were analyzed alongside EEG features.

Clinical assessments captured depressive symptoms, anxiety, sleep quality, trauma history, and coping strategies.

Both supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques were applied to compare individuals with Major Depressive Disorder and healthy controls.

Advanced interpretability methods were used to understand feature contributions.

This multi-modal, data-driven approach aims to enhance the objectivity and sensitivity of depression diagnosis.
